Strategic Thesis
Where the Nesting Points

CDI's current work lives primarily in the individual dimensions — Q1 (self-awareness and perspective-taking) and Q2 (behavioral skills). This is where the evidence base is deepest, the product is strongest, and the institutional flywheel is already turning. Right now, in early 2026, the team is heads-down on two parallel efforts: stabilizing the existing platform while migrating to a new one — with AI-enhanced Perspectives (coaching moments integrated into select activities) piloting with select partners in September.

But the framework itself tells us where to go next. If we each hold a unique perspective is the seed, and if the nesting expresses that seed at every altitude, then CDI's principles require us to address what happens when perspectives meet — the intersubjective — and what structures shape that encounter — the systemic. The constraint is sequencing: nothing new launches until the foundation is solid. June 2026 opens the planning window — in parallel with monitoring what the AI features teach us and preparing for full platform migration in H1 2027.

The collective dimensions name where the nesting points next. Not because expansion is strategically convenient, but because the architecture demands it. And not before the foundation can hold the weight.

The Measurement Shift: From Participants to Dialogues

CDI currently measures individual learners — intellectual humility, perspective-taking, openness. These remain essential. But Encounter demands something new: measurement of the dialogue itself. Not "Did each person improve?" but "What happened between the perspectives? Did the quality of encounter improve?"

The intersubjective research program will pilot measurement across four dimensions:

  • Conversation Structure: Participation equity (% speaking turns by viewpoint), question prevalence (authentic questions vs. rhetorical), turn-taking reciprocity, silence patterns
  • Cognitive Dynamics: Perspective uptake (moments where a speaker incorporates another's view), reframing (shift from binary to nuanced framing), shared understanding emergence
  • Relational Climate: Epistemic trust (group believes partners are genuine), vulnerability signals, tone shifts across the dialogue arc, repair moments after ruptures
  • Collective Outcomes: Perspective integration (group-level synthesis beyond individual views), curiosity increase, conflict engagement quality, actionable shared commitments

Measurement approaches: (1) Observational coding by facilitators and research partners, (2) AI conversation analytics — real-time facilitator alerts and post-session pattern extraction, (3) Participant self-report integrated into Encounter's reflection layer, (4) Longitudinal dialogue portfolio tracking across sessions.

2026 outcomes: Working definition of "dialogue quality," observational coding protocol, AI analytics prototype, facilitator rubric. 2027 outcomes: Baseline metrics across 50+ partner dialogues, live AI dashboard in Encounter, validation that dialogue quality correlates with institutional culture change.

Nesting check ✓ — The Meta-Perspective (Alt 5) says we each hold a unique perspective. If that's true, then what happens when those perspectives meet is not peripheral — it's implicit in the seed. Measuring the dialogue — not just the dialoguers — is the research method nested in the philosophy. This scoping makes explicit what the nesting already contains, without overcommitting resources before the platform foundation is solid.

Ship Encounter — CDI's Intersubjective Product
A two-layer platform: facilitator-facing dialogue mapping + participant-facing shared reflection. CDI's first product designed for what happens between perspectives.
Q3 Alt 1–3
Built on 2026 intersubjective research, Encounter is a two-layer product addressing group dialogue dynamics — not what each individual brings, but the quality of what emerges when perspectives meet.

Facilitator Layer: A real-time dialogue dashboard that maps group dynamics during facilitated sessions. Who's been heard and who hasn't. Where tension concentrates. Which perspectives are missing from the room. AI-generated suggested interventions ("The group hasn't engaged the economic framing — consider surfacing it"). Post-session analytics: conversation flow visualization, participation equity metrics, moments of genuine perspective uptake flagged for facilitator debrief.

Participant Layer: After each dialogue session, participants access a shared reflection space. An AI-synthesized "encounter map" shows what the group explored, where it got stuck, and what perspectives emerged. Participants annotate their own experience onto the map — "I felt defensive here," "This is where I started to understand the other view." Over time, these maps build a personal dialogue portfolio that surfaces patterns in how each person encounters difference.

The two layers create a feedback loop: facilitator observations inform participant reflections, and participant annotations improve facilitator intelligence for the next session.
  • Encounter MVP launched with 10+ institutional partners
  • Facilitator dashboard: real-time dialogue mapping validated (accuracy > 75% vs. human coding)
  • Participant reflection space: engagement > 60% post-session completion rate
  • Dialogue-level outcome metrics validated against 2026 research baseline:
    • Perspective uptake: baseline established for cross-institutional comparison
    • Participation equity: target > 30% any single viewpoint (vs. benchmark 15%)
    • Question prevalence: > 25% of turns are authentic questions
    • Collective reframing: documented in 60%+ of facilitated dialogue sessions
    • Epistemic trust: measured via post-dialogue survey (> 4.2/5.0)
  • Integration pathway with Perspectives (individual → group → individual reflection cycle)
Nesting check ✓ — The Mission (Alt 3) is to create conditions for constructive dialogue. Individual skill is necessary but insufficient — the conditions include the relational space itself. Encounter serves the Mission more fully than individual-only tools can. The two-layer design mirrors the nesting: inner posture (participant reflection) expressed through outer structure (facilitator tools).

Decision Criteria
Six Strategic Principles

Every initiative in this roadmap — and every decision that follows it — is tested against these principles. They are the nesting applied to strategy itself.

Does This Nest?
Every initiative must cohere with the layers it grows from. If a product decision can't be traced through Conditions → Mission → Perspective → Meta-Perspective, it doesn't belong.
Principled Humility
Grounded in conviction, rooted in curiosity. We hold strong positions — but as posture, not identity. The roadmap itself is held provisionally.
Institutional Humility
CDI builds the table, not sits at its head. We practice what we teach: holding our own framework the way we ask others to hold theirs.
Education ⇄ Research
Neither drives; both enable. Research without education is sterile. Education without research is stagnant. The flywheel is the engine.
Five Altitudes as Coherence Check
Meta-Perspective → Perspective → Mission → Conditions → Product. Skip a level and coherence fractures. The altitude stack is the integrity test.
Measure the Dialogue, Not Just the Dialoguers
